Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve

Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve is the first Singapore's ASEAN Heritage Park

It was awarded a certificate by Wetlands International, marking the reserve's formal entry into the East Asian-Australasian Shorebird Site Network

In 2016, the park includes a 202ha of mangroves, mudflats, ponds and forests

Migratory Bird Trail

Forest Trail

The Forest Trail offers visitors the chance to experience the sights and sounds of the reserve up close, and learn more about the plants and wildlife which are unique to the reserve.

When you trek along the boardwalk you will be brought to many mangroves. But the real challenge for the kids will be trying to pinpoint the elusive mudskipper - which you will manage to do so after some time!

Locate Them

Mid Canopy Walk

Mid-canopy Walk immerses visitors in the understory of a secondary forest through an elevated, 120m-long boardwalk, where they can spot birds and insects like cicadas that reside in the mid-canopy region and forest floor

Introduction

This is an app about the Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve.

It is First Singapore's ASEAN Heritage Park.

It was awarded a certificate by Wetlands International, marking the reserve's formal entry into the East Asian-Australasian Shorebird Site Network. As from 2016, the park includes a 202 hectares of mangroves, mudflats, ponds and forests.

Coastal trail Content

Visitor Center

The visitor center features as an interactive educational tools ranging from multimedia games, live cameras to a mangrove gallery featuring different kinds of information to the visitors.

It also has facilities such as toilets, rest shelter, multi-purpose rooms, and a first-aid center.

Before we start, remember to empty your bladder here, because it's gonna be a long walk!

KingFisher Pod

The Kingfisher pod is the highest elevated pod out of the five.

It provides a scenic view of the mangrove at a canopy level. BEWARE, You might spot crocodiles too.

Do not go near the mud area, watch out for those predators!!

Main Bridge

Before we start going to the actual trail itself, stop at the Main Bridge to see a large school of fishes. At the moderate tide, the Kingfisher will be down there to hunt for fishes. It's a great site to see the eco-system here and the beauty of these creatures
